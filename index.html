This strategy attempts to capture the intuition that many bad actions are irreversible. Saving a life is relatively easier to undo, whereas causing a death is harder to undo. No actions are truly reversible, but requiring them to be approximately reversible seems intuitively to put an upper bound on how bad it can be, even if we rarely or never press the undo button. This provides some intuition for the idea that we would not just be constantly undoing the agent’s actions in this paradigm—an action that is approximately undo-able is likelier to be acceptable to us.

Difficulty Measures

Three metrics are proposed here for measuring the difficulty of moving from one state to another (for an agent with action space ): time-difficulty, probability-difficulty, and money-difficulty.
